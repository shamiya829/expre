Case study • Food

Time Out Market

Editorial curation as an operating model: selection, consistency, and programming create repeat visits.

Retail playbook Programming principle Back to library

At a glance

  • Experience strategy Curated food hall + event energy
  • Tenant mix Chef-driven stalls + bar + community tables
  • Design elements Clear flow, seating ladder, wayfinding
  • Financial logic High utilization + repeatable format
Developer lessons

What to steal

  • Curation is the moat; brand is built on selection quality.
  • Operations create consistency (clean, fast, clear).
  • Community seating supports groups and dwell.
  • Programming keeps it fresh without format reinvention.

Background

Time Out Market applies editorial curation to food hall development, positioning the hall as a destination rather than a convenience amenity.

Experience strategy

Make selection feel trustworthy and “worth the trip,” then support repeat visits through events, seasonal rotations, and a social atmosphere.

Tenant mix

Chef-driven stalls + beverage program + dessert/late-night options, arranged to create a loop and avoid dead corners.

Design elements

  • Clear ordering and pickup logic (reduce friction tax).
  • Seating ladder: quick seats to linger zones.
  • Acoustic planning so social energy doesn’t become discomfort.

Financial logic

Utilization and throughput are key; curated demand supports tenant performance and brand expansion as a repeatable concept.

Lessons developers can apply

  • Curation is ongoing operations (standards and refresh), not just leasing.
  • Underwrite staffing and cleaning as brand protection, not optional opex.
  • Design queue and pickup flow as part of the experience.
