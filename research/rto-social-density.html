Research brief

RTO reality: design for social density, not desks

If the office is optional, the building has to earn the commute: community, energy, and service.

Experiential office playbook Social design All research

The shift

Office demand is increasingly driven by social and experiential value: collaboration, culture, and belonging—more than workstation utility.

Design moves that matter

  • Make arrival feel like a venue: quick, welcoming, and brand-clear.
  • Design a ladder of social spaces: public lounge → semi-private → reservable.
  • Control acoustics and glare so social spaces are comfortable.

Operating moves that matter

  • Hire for community: programming, partnerships, and tenant success.
  • Adopt hospitality-level service standards and service recovery.
  • Measure utilization, attendance proxies, and tenant feedback monthly.
