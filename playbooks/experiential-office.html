Playbook • Office

Experiential office

Office demand is pulled by experience: social energy, hospitality service, and amenity-as-a-system—then measured in attendance and renewals.

Read the RTO brief Hospitality principle All playbooks

What makes the experience work

  • Arrival theater Fast, welcoming, brand-reinforcing
  • Social density Spaces for collisions and groups
  • Service layer Concierge, rooms, events, vendor enablement
  • Third spaces Work + coffee + lounge without friction

Operating model notes

  • Community manager Programming and tenant success
  • Amenity bookings Rooms, events, services
  • Experience KPIs Attendance, utilization, NPS, retention
  • Partnerships Wellness, food, learning, networking

Revenue and NOI levers

Experience drives leasing and retention: better attendance outcomes for tenants translate into stronger demand for space.

Lever Experience mechanism How to execute
Lease renewals Tenant success Service standards, programming, amenities that work
Market rents Premium experience Arrival + hospitality + social spaces
Ancillary income Services Event space, memberships, food, wellness partnerships
Operational efficiency Measured flow Utilization data for staffing and cleaning
Key design elements

Design for choice

  • Arrival sequence that feels like a venue.
  • Social spaces with acoustic control (no “loud lobby”).
  • Mix of public, semi-private, and reservable zones.
  • Wayfinding that supports visitors and events.
Common mistakes
  • Amenities without staffing, booking flow, or standards.
  • Social spaces that are uncomfortable (glare, noise).
  • Over-indexing on tech screens instead of service.
  • No plan for community programming.
