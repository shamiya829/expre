Playbook • Retail

Experiential retail

Retail wins when it feels like discovery and belonging—then converts attention into spending through flow, programming, and tenant adjacency.

Download tools See case studies All playbooks

What makes the experience work

  • Discovery loop Clear path + surprises + reasons to pause
  • Social logic Group-friendly seating + shareable moments
  • F&B support Food/coffee as dwell and repeat engine
  • Programming rhythm Events that feel native, not “marketing”

Operating model notes

  • Tenant enablement Support pop-ups, collabs, and drops
  • Guest services Clean, safe, easy wayfinding
  • Merch + moments Place conversion points near dwell nodes
  • Measurement Track dwell, revisits, queue time, NPS

Revenue and NOI levers

Retail economics improve when experience increases time-on-site and conversion, supporting stronger tenant sales and rent outcomes.

Lever Experience mechanism How to execute
Sales productivity Dwell time + discovery Looping circulation, pause nodes, clear anchors
Rent support Stable footfall Programming calendar and co-marketing norms
Tenant retention Brand lift Service standards + clean public realm + safety
Ancillary revenue Events + sponsorship Event infrastructure and partner pipeline
Tenant mix strategy

Build a narrative mix

  • Magnet experiences: flagship F&B, entertainment, curated markets.
  • Staples: daily needs that increase visit frequency.
  • Discovery brands: rotating pop-ups, local creators, limited drops.
  • Social spend: bars, dessert, late-night formats near dwell nodes.
Common mistakes
  • “Pretty corridor” with no reason to stop.
  • Too much seating without food/service and shade.
  • Anchors that don’t connect to a loop (dead ends).
  • Activation with no infrastructure or budget.

Key design elements

Design for behavior: arrival → discovery → dwell → conversion → return.

Flow

Loops + sightlines

Make the experience legible while keeping surprise.

Dwell

Pause nodes

Micro-plazas, seating variety, and shade + comfort.

Conversion

Spend points

Place F&B, merch, and kiosks near natural dwell.

Demographic alignment

Match experience intensity to the real patterns of your trade area.

High-intensity markets

Tourism, young professionals, and destination shoppers support frequent drops, nightlife, and ticketed activations.

Family + convenience markets

Focus on comfort, safe public realm, kid-friendly rituals, and weekly programming that builds habit.
