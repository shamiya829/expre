Playbook • Mixed-use

Mixed-use / lifestyle centers

The job is to create a district: legible gateways, a walk loop, and a public realm that can host programming year-round.

Download tools See case studies All playbooks

What makes the experience work

  • Legible district Gateways, sub-areas, clear identity
  • Walk loop Continuous circulation + “reasons to turn”
  • Calendar Weekly rhythms + seasonal signature moments
  • Edges Activated ground floors and comfortable plazas

Operating model notes

  • District manager Programming + partners + standards
  • Tenant collaboration Shared events, cross-promotions
  • Guest services Wayfinding, safety, cleaning, parking flow
  • Measurement Footfall by node, dwell, event conversion

Revenue and NOI levers

District performance comes from stable demand, higher capture, and better leasing outcomes across uses.

Lever Experience mechanism How to execute
Leasing velocity Clear identity + footfall Brand narrative + tenant adjacency and anchors
Rent premiums Desirable public realm Comfort, safety, shade, seating density, programming
Cross-use capture Stay longer Walk loop + layered reasons to dwell (food, events, retail)
Ancillary income Events + sponsorship Event-ready plazas, ticketing, brand partnerships
Tenant mix strategy

Compose a day-to-night ladder

  • Daytime anchors: coffee, wellness, services, family-friendly uses.
  • Evening anchors: social games, bars, destination dining.
  • Public realm support: vendors, markets, pop-ups.
  • Residential + office synergy: design frictionless cross-use movement.
Common mistakes
  • Plazas with no shade, seating, or reason-to-stay density.
  • Parking/arrival that feels hostile or confusing.
  • “Main street” with dead ground-floor edges.
  • Programming that isn’t operationally supported.

Key design elements

Make the district readable and comfortable, with flexible spaces that can host people and content.

Gateways

Arrival sequences

Clear entry moments that signal “you’re here.”

Loop

Continuous walk

Design loops, not corridors and cul-de-sacs.

Event-ready

Plazas with infrastructure

Power, storage, access, and acoustic planning.
