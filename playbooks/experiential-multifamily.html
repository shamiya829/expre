Playbook • Multifamily

Experiential multifamily

The experience is retention. Design and operations should create routine, belonging, and recovery—then translate that into renewals and premiums.

Download tools Hospitality principle All playbooks

What makes the experience work

  • Hospitality layer Concierge mindset, not a leasing desk
  • Routine design Spaces residents use weekly, not yearly
  • Wellness stack Movement + recovery + calm
  • Community rhythm Events that build weak ties into belonging

Operating model notes

  • Community manager Programming, partners, content
  • Service standards Fast response + consistent cleanliness
  • Experience KPIs Renewal rate, amenity utilization, NPS
  • Vendor ecosystem Wellness, pets, tutoring, mobility

Revenue and NOI levers

In multifamily, experience primarily monetizes through retention, premiums, and ancillary revenue.

Lever Experience mechanism How to execute
Renewals / turnover cost Belonging + routine Weekly programming, third spaces, service standards
Rent premium Perceived quality Hospitality-grade lobbies, wellness stack, quiet recovery
Amenity utilization Ease + comfort Right-sizing, acoustics, reservable zones
Ancillary revenue Services Classes, memberships, pet services, mobility packages
Key design elements

Design for routine

  • Lobby as social living room (not a pass-through).
  • Work-from-home spaces with acoustic zoning.
  • Wellness loop: movement + recovery + outdoor comfort.
  • Resident-visible amenity edges that animate the ground floor.
Common mistakes
  • Amenities that look good but feel bad (noise, glare, overcrowding).
  • Programming without partners or staffing.
  • Over-privatizing spaces that could build community.
  • No measurement: “we think they like it.”
